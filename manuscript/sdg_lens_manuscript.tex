\begin{abstract}
SDG Lens classifies text from the SDGi corpus into the 17 United Nations
Sustainable Development Goals. The marker-facing pipeline trains a MiniLM-based
multi-label neural model and a TF--IDF baseline over three random seeds and two
training-set sizes, evaluates all artifacts on a fixed held-out test sample, and
reports mean and standard deviation across seeds.
\end{abstract}

\section{Pipeline}

The full reproducible command is:

\begin{verbatim}
python main.py sweep
\end{verbatim}

The pipeline writes trained artifacts to \texttt{artifacts/}, evaluation results
to \texttt{results/}, manuscript-ready charts and tables to \texttt{outputs/},
and this compiled PDF to \texttt{manuscript/}.

\section{Interpretability Note}

The neural model records attention-based token examples as a proxy explanation.
These should be interpreted as inspection aids rather than validated human
rationales because the SDGi corpus provides document-level SDG labels, not
token-level rationale annotations.
